Precious Pastimes

Liesel’s Soccer Injury

June 5, 1942 Rudy Steiner

While playing soccer on the street, Klaus Behrig collided with Liesel, injuring Liesel’s knee. Liesel asked me to call Herr Hubermann, which I did. He took her inside.

I went to check on her some time later and although her injury was fine, she was acting very worried. It seems the stealing has gotten to her.

The Jesse Owens Race

Aug 10, 1936 Rudy Steiner

Yesterday, a Black American runner named Jesse Owens won his fourth gold medal in this year’s Olympics. At night, when no one was looking, I coated myself in black with charcoal and snuck out of the house. I biked down to Hubert Oval and ran his race, the 4 x 100m relay.

The crowd was roaring my name. I was Jesse Owens. I was Jesse Owens in every stride I took of that race - until my dad showed up. For some reason, he seemed less upset that I had snuck out to run, and more upset about the charcoal smeared on my face. He said something about how I was lucky to be German, and not black or Jewish. What’s wrong with being black or Jewish?
